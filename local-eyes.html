Local Eyes – Real Experiences from Real People

Hello my Pioneer friend,

I would like to share with you an early idea that I am building and would love to hear your honest thoughts and perspective.

The idea is called Local Eyes.

The concept is simple:

When people travel to a new place, they often search online for information. However, much of the available content today may come from advertising, commercial interests, or general information that does not always reflect real-life situations.

I believe there is another valuable source of information: real experiences from real people who have actually been there.

For example:

  • Where can travelers experience local food at a reasonable cost?
  • Which places feel safe and comfortable?
  • What interesting experiences are available beyond typical tourist recommendations?
  • What practical things should visitors know before coming to a new area?

The idea is to create a community where local people can share what they have personally experienced.

The Core Principles

The most important principle is authenticity:

  • No fake reviews.
  • No invented experiences.
  • No misleading information.
  • No paid promotions presented as personal opinions.
  • No exaggeration beyond what has actually been experienced.

If someone shares information, it should come from something they personally saw, visited, or experienced.

The purpose is not to replace professional travel services. It is to create a place where people can connect through honest experiences and local knowledge.

An Early Testnet dApp Experiment

I am currently building a very early version of this idea as a Testnet dApp on Pi Network, following the community movement around creating and experimenting with dApp ideas through Vibe Coding.

At this stage, it is only a small prototype.

I am learning step by step, improving the idea gradually, and using technology as a tool to support the community concept.

The technology is not the most important part.

The most important part is building trust through real human experiences.

How I Imagine It Working

Traveler → "I want to visit your area. Can you share your real experience?"
Local → "I have been there. This is what I saw, this is what I experienced, and these are my personal observations."

The value comes from honest sharing between people.

This Is Not a Travel Business

I would like to clearly clarify:

  • This is not a travel agency.
  • This is not a tour service.
  • This is not a paid consulting service.

There are:

  • No service fees.
  • No paid reviews.
  • No selling of rankings or recommendations.
  • No obligation for anyone to participate.

The idea is based on freely sharing useful experiences.

If someone finds the information helpful and wants to support a contributor voluntarily, that is their personal choice. The core value remains the sharing of authentic experiences.

Learning Through Community Feedback

At this early stage, I am simply sharing this idea with other Pioneers around the world to understand different perspectives.

I would like to hear your thoughts:

  • Would you find this type of local experience-sharing platform useful when traveling?
  • Would you trust recommendations from real local people more than commercial travel content?
  • What features would make you feel comfortable using such a platform?
  • What challenges do you think this idea may face?

This is only the beginning of a long journey.

I am not trying to build everything at once. I am starting with a small idea, testing it, listening to feedback, and improving gradually.
